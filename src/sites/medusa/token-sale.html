In Greek mythology, Medusa was a monster, a Gorgon, generally described as a winged humanoid female with living venomous snakes in place of hair. Gazers upon her hideous face would turn to stone.

In classical antiquity the image of the head of Medusa appeared in the evil-averting device known as the Gorgoneion.. Olympian deities, Athena and Zeus, both are said to have worn the gorgoneion as a protective pendant.

Medusa was beheaded by the hero Perseus, who thereafter used her head, which retained its ability to turn onlookers to stone, as a weapon until he gave it to the goddess Athena to place on her shield.

Since that time, and through the ages the use of Medusa’s image has become a symbol which protects the bearer from his enemies, and for the same reason a good luck charm.
